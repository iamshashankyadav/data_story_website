The Late Evening Craving 🍕📦

It was a long day, and I was exhausted. The evening had just set in, and I thought a warm, delicious meal would be the perfect way to unwind.there fore i went to miss and saw boring mix veg which ruined my mind so i thought of ordering food online.But there was a problem—I had no idea which app would deliver my food the fastest. I had been burned before—waiting for what felt like an eternity for my food to arrive, only to receive it cold and disappointing. This time, I wanted to be smart about it so i collected some data of past order from my phone and my freinds phone and done analysis on it.

Which App is the Fastest? 🚀

Looking at the average delivery times, I found that:

  • ✅ Domino’s: Fastest (~36 minutes).
  • ⚡ Swiggy: Decent (~42 minutes).
  • 🐢 Zomato: Slowest (~51 minutes).

If I was starving and needed food fast, Domino’s was the way to go. But if I wanted more variety, Swiggy was still a decent option.

Does Time of Day Matter? ⏳

Turns out, it does! Ordering in the evening meant the longest wait time may due to high demand:

  • 🌇 Evening (49 min) → Worst time to order, peak rush!
  • ⏳ Afternoon (39 min) → Quick deliveries, fewer orders.
  • ☀️ Morning (44 min) → Slower, but manageable.
  • 🌙 Night (41 min) → Better than evening but still slower.

Distance & Its Impact 📏

I knew distance mattered, but how much? Looking at my analysis:

Short distances (1-3 km) → Fast deliveries.
Long distances (5+ km) → Extra 15-20 minutes added.

Predicting My Delivery Time 🤖

But then, a thought struck me— 💡 What if I could predict the exact delivery time just by knowing the restaurant distance, the app, and the time of day?

I decided to train multiple machine learning models to predict delivery time:

  • ✅ Linear Regression - For a basic understanding.
  • 🌲 Random Forest - To capture non-linear relationships.
  • 🚀 Gradient Boosting (XGBoost) - To refine the predictions.
  • 🧠 Polynomial Regression (Degree 3) - To model complex patterns.
  • 🤖 Ensemble Model (Voting Regressor) - Combining all models for accuracy.
  • ✅ mean absolute error - 2.8376205590704178.
  • 📏 R1 score - 0.8524385950327058.

The Final Decision 🎯

Best App? Domino’s (fastest).
Best Strategy? Pick a restaurant within 3 km.
Best Time? Not now—but if I had ordered in the afternoon, I could have saved 10 minutes!

The Result ✅

Domino’s delivered in 35 minutes—exactly as predicted. Now, I don’t order blindly anymore. I predict my delivery time before ordering and choose the best option accordingly! 🚀
